⚠
A note on accuracy Food desert designations follow strict USDA criteria: census tracts where 20%+ of residents live below the poverty line AND 33%+ live more than 1 mile from a supermarket. Counter-intuitively, areas like Federal Way and Tukwila — despite serving large immigrant populations — are not classified as food deserts because they have multiple grocery options (Costco, Fred Meyer, Walmart, Trader Joe's, plus specialty markets). The actual King County food deserts are Skyway/Bryn Mawr, White Center, parts of Delridge, and parts of Auburn, where supermarket access is structurally limited.

Limitations & next steps

USDA's Food Access Research Atlas updates infrequently (last full refresh 2019), so 2026 figures extrapolate from current Census ACS. The diabetes-food access correlation here is observational and doesn't control for ethnicity, age, or activity levels — refinements would require IRB-approved access to clinical records. The food desert classification is also imperfect: South King County areas like Federal Way have many grocery stores but still serve communities with cultural food access gaps that USDA criteria don't capture. Future work should integrate KC Public Health hospital admissions data, SNAP enrollment APIs, and culturally specific food access surveys.
