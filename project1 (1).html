Key findings04 insights
Finding 01
Federal Way is the critical gap zone
The highest concentration of East African and Latin American immigrants in King County has zero refugee-serving clinics within a 5-mile radius. Closest equivalent care is 12+ miles north in Rainier Valley.
Finding 02
Language is the #1 access barrier
Only 4 of 14 refugee-serving clinics offer Somali-language support, despite Somali residents being the largest refugee group in King County with a 38% uninsured rate.
Finding 03
Northgate & Rainier Valley are anchor hubs
These two areas have the strongest existing infrastructure. Expanding telehealth and multilingual intake from these hubs could extend coverage to surrounding underserved zip codes cost-effectively.
Finding 04
Documentation fear compounds cost barriers
18% of surveyed immigrants cite fear of documentation requirements as a primary reason for avoiding care, even when legally eligible for Apple Health or refugee programs.
Recommended IS interventions04 actions
Intervention 01
Multilingual digital intake
Deploy forms in Somali, Spanish, Vietnamese at all 14 clinics. Estimated ~40% reduction in first-visit drop-off based on similar implementations elsewhere.
Intervention 02
Mobile clinic routes
Rotating 2x weekly mobile clinic in Federal Way, Tukwila, SeaTac — addresses geographic gap without capital cost of a new fixed facility.
Intervention 03
Community health navigators
Train bilingual community members to bridge the documentation-fear gap and connect families to Apple Health and state refugee coverage programs.
Intervention 04
IS utilization dashboard
Internal tool tracking no-shows, language requests, and zip code origins for data-driven resource allocation across the county system.
Limitations & next steps

This analysis relies on aggregated state-level Census data combined with KC-specific community health surveys. Future iterations should pull individual-tract data via the Census Bureau's TIGER/Line shapefiles for higher-resolution mapping, and integrate live KC Public Health clinic capacity APIs once available. The recommendations are directional rather than prescriptive — implementation would require partnership with affected communities and validation by clinical operations leaders.
