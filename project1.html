Key findings
01
Federal Way is the critical gap
Highest concentration of East African and Latin American immigrants with zero refugee-serving clinics within 5 miles.
02
Language is the #1 barrier
Only 4 of 14 refugee clinics offer Somali-language support despite Somali residents being the largest refugee group in KC.
03
Northgate & Rainier Valley are hubs
Expanding telehealth and multilingual intake at these two existing hubs could extend coverage cost-effectively.
Recommended IS interventions
Intervention 01
Multilingual digital intake
Deploy forms in Somali, Spanish, Vietnamese at all 14 clinics. Estimated ~40% reduction in first-visit drop-off.
Intervention 02
Mobile clinic routes
Rotating 2x weekly mobile clinic in Federal Way, Tukwila, SeaTac — no new fixed facility required.
Intervention 03
Community health navigators
Train bilingual community members to bridge documentation-fear gap and connect families to Apple Health.
Intervention 04
IS utilization dashboard
Track no-shows, language requests, zip code origins for data-driven resource allocation across the county.
