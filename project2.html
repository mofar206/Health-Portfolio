Key findings04 insights
Finding 01
South KC has zero accessible behavioral health facilities
Federal Way and Tukwila — home to KC's largest immigrant populations — have no outpatient mental health clinics accepting Medicaid within their boundaries.
Finding 02
Deserts overlap with poverty + immigrant density
A near-perfect correlation exists between areas with 0 providers per 10k residents and census tracts with poverty rates above 20% and foreign-born populations above 30%.
Finding 03
Telehealth closes only part of the gap
Language barriers and lack of devices/broadband mean telehealth reaches only ~40% of desert-zone residents who need it — meaningful but insufficient alone.
Finding 04
Centralized IS referral could cut wait times
A shared referral and waitlist tracking system across KC behavioral health providers could reduce average wait time from 6 weeks to under 2 with existing resources.
Limitations & next steps

Provider counts are derived from SAMHSA's public Treatment Locator, which doesn't always reflect facility-level capacity (waitlists, intake pauses, accepted insurance). Future iterations should integrate KC Public Health survey data and direct provider scheduling APIs where available. Telehealth coverage estimates draw on broadband access data from the FCC and may overstate effective reach.
