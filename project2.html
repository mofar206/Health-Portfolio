Key findings
Finding 01
South KC has zero accessible behavioral health facilities
Federal Way and Tukwila — home to KC's largest immigrant populations — have no outpatient mental health clinics accepting Medicaid.
Finding 02
Deserts overlap with poverty and immigrant density
A near-perfect correlation exists between areas with 0 providers per 10k residents and census tracts with poverty rates above 20% and foreign-born populations above 30%.
Finding 03
Telehealth closes only part of the gap
Language barriers and lack of devices/broadband mean telehealth reaches only ~40% of desert-zone residents who need it.
Finding 04
Centralized IS referral system could cut wait times
A shared referral and waitlist tracking system could reduce average wait from 6 weeks to under 2 with existing provider resources.
